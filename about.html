About

A bit more about me.

I’m Elijah — a student researcher and musician who grew up translating: between Spanish and English, between adults and institutions, between complex systems and the people most affected by them. Medical forms, legal documents, and school emails came to our house in one language; my job was to turn them into something my family could act on.

That role quietly shaped how I think about science and art. Research feels incomplete if the people it’s about can’t access it. Music feels incomplete if it doesn’t leave room for other people’s stories. So whether I’m writing about stress pathways in cancer or playing piano in a rehearsal room, I’m always thinking about translation and care.

This website is a small snapshot of that work in progress: the projects that have taught me something, the images and sounds that stayed with me, and the questions I’m still trying to ask well.

Current interests
  • Stress and inflammation in reproductive cancers
  • Ecology and climate resilience in Northern California
  • Music as a practice of attention and community
  • Making complex systems legible to the people they affect

Outside of school
  • Playing piano (and saying yes to too many ensembles)
  • Taking photos at concerts, games, and gatherings
  • Spending time with family and the communities that raised me

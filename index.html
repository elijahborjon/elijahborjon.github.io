Research

Biology as a language of stress and adaptation.

My work spans cancer biology, stress pathways, and ecology—from CXCR2-dependent inflammation in reproductive cancers to monitoring butterflies as indicators of ecosystem health.

Cancer Biology · Pioneer Academics

CXCR2-mediated stress & inflammation in Central American reproductive cancers

Literature-based investigation of how chronic stress, migration, and under-resourced health systems intersect with CXCR2 signaling in cervical and ovarian cancers.

Manuscript archived on Zenodo · remote mentorship with Prof. Engda Hagos.
Ecology · Pepperwood Preserve

Butterflies as living climate sensors

Developed open-source field guides for local students, using butterfly presence and behavior as an accessible entry into climate change, habitat loss, and data literacy.

Community science · outdoor education · Spanish-English materials.
Future Directions

Stress biology across cells, bodies, and communities

Ongoing interests include maternal stress, neuroinflammation, and how social determinants of health map onto molecular pathways in marginalized communities.

Seeking labs that bridge bench science with public health and policy.
Music

Holding complexity in sound.

Music is where I practice the kind of attention I want to bring into the lab—patient, precise, and deeply human. I’ve studied piano for over thirteen years and clarinet and voice through chamber groups and choir.

Piano · 13+ years
Repertoire from Debussy to Schubert; classical focus with jazz explorations.
Preparing dual-track auditions and exploring how harmony mirrors biological systems: tension, release, and unexpected resolutions.
Choir & Chamber Ensembles
Tenor section work · touring performances · community concerts.
Ensemble music has taught me how to listen laterally—adjusting to others in real time, the way cells respond to signals in a shared environment.
Sound as a way of knowing
Improvisation · arranging · mentorship.
I think of each piece like a small experiment: constraints, hypotheses, variations, and the emotional data we collect by playing.

About

Why I do this work.

I grew up translating—between languages, systems, and people. Medical forms, legal documents, and school emails arrived at our house in English; my job was to turn them into something my family could act on.

That experience shaped how I see science and music: both are powerful, but only if they’re shared in a way others can actually use. This site is one attempt at that—an archive of work at the edge of what I know so far.

Snapshot

Current interests:

  • Inflammation and stress pathways in cancer and brain health
  • Ecology and climate resilience in Northern California
  • Music education as a tool for access and community building
  • Bridging lab science with policy, design, and storytelling
Long term, I hope to work where rigorous biology, creative communication, and community care meet in the same room.
